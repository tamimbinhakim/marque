# eIDAS 2.0 ↔ Marque compliance mapping

> Side-by-side table of [Regulation (EU) 2024/1183](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1183/oj) (eIDAS 2.0 — amending Regulation (EU) 910/2014; entered into force 20 May 2024; implementing acts land through 2026–2027), the relevant ETSI ESI standards, and the Marque specification sections that implement or profile against each requirement.
>
> **Intended audience.** Qualified Trust Service Providers (QTSPs), national supervisory bodies maintaining trust lists, eIDAS-conformance auditors, and national-scale procurement teams evaluating Marque as a QERDS substrate.
>
> **Status.** Pre-conformance. This mapping is declarative: it asserts which Marque sections are intended to satisfy which eIDAS requirements. Formal conformance assessment against [ETSI EN 319 521](https://www.etsi.org/deliver/etsi_en/319500_319599/319521/) (ERDS provider policy and security requirements) and the [ETSI TS 119 524-1/-2](https://www.etsi.org/deliver/etsi_ts/119500_119599/) conformance test suites for ERDS is a Phase-2 roadmap milestone (see [`docs/roadmap.md`](../roadmap.md)).
>
> **Scope.** This document covers only the **Electronic Registered Delivery Services (ERDS)** and **Qualified Electronic Registered Delivery Services (QERDS)** provisions of eIDAS 2.0, plus the adjacent qualified-timestamp and qualified-certificate provisions that Qualified-tier Marque depends on. It does not cover electronic-signature (eIDAS Chapter III Section 4) or electronic-seal (Section 5) provisions as standalone services; Marque integrates qualified signatures and seals by reference when carried inside `core.signed_action` content blocks.

## How to read this mapping

Each row below pairs an eIDAS article or ETSI clause with the Marque specification sections and schema artifacts that implement it. Three status marks:

- **✅ Met** — the Marque specification explicitly satisfies the requirement; implementation only.
- **🟡 Profiled** — the Marque specification defines a profile or binding; formal conformance assessment required.
- **⚠ Deferred** — the Marque specification acknowledges the requirement and defers to a companion draft or future MIP.

## 1. eIDAS 2.0 — ERDS provisions

Electronic Registered Delivery Services, Regulation (EU) 2024/1183, Chapter III, Section 7.

| ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ----------- |
| **Article 43(1)** — ERDS legal effects                                                                                                                | Data sent and received via ERDS shall not be denied legal effect or admissibility.                                                      | Attested tier (`auth_mode = SIGNED`) produces non-repudiable sender signature + RFC 3161 timestamp + OpenTimestamps anchor. See [`spec/protocol/07-legal-proof.md`](../../spec/protocol/07-legal-proof.md). | 🟡 Profiled |
| **Article 43(2)(a)** — identification of sender                                                                                                       | ERDS shall ensure the identification of the sender with a high level of confidence.                                                     | `did:mail` + DSK signature chaining to IRK; KEL-verified. See [`spec/protocol/02-identity.md`](../../spec/protocol/02-identity.md).                                            | ✅ Met      |
| **Article 43(2)(b)** — identification of addressee                                                                                                    | ERDS shall ensure the identification of the addressee.                                                                                  | Recipient `did:mail` resolution at deposit; proof of delivery binds recipient DID.                                                                                             | ✅ Met      |
| **Article 43(2)(c)** — integrity of data                                                                                                              | Data sent shall be secured against the risk of loss, theft, damage, or any unauthorized alterations.                                    | MLS RFC 9420 AEAD + envelope BLAKE3 `content_id` bound into signature. See [`spec/protocol/05-cryptography.md`](../../spec/protocol/05-cryptography.md).                       | ✅ Met      |

| **Article 43(2)(e)** — tamper-evident trace                                                                                                           | Any change to the data shall be indicated.                                                                                              | Envelope signature + BLAKE3 content hash + MLS transcript hash.                                                                                                                | ✅ Met      |

## 2. eIDAS 2.0 — QERDS provisions

Qualified Electronic Registered Delivery Services, Regulation (EU) 2024/1183, Chapter III, Section 7, Article 44.

| eIDAS provision                                                                                                                 | Requirement summary                                                                                                            | Marque implementation                                                                                                                                                                        | Status      |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------- |
| **Article 44(1)(a)** — provided by one or more QTSPs                                                                            | A QERDS is provided by one or more QTSPs.                                                                                       | Qualified tier REQUIRES a QTSP co-signature in the `ProofEnvelope`. The QTSP is listed in the `proof_envelope.qtsp_cert` field. See [`spec/protocol/07-legal-proof.md`](../../spec/protocol/07-legal-proof.md) §7.3. | 🟡 Profiled |
| **Article 44(1)(b)** — high level of confidence in sender identification                                                        | Sender identification with a high level of confidence.                                                                         | Qualified certificate issued by QTSP binding `did:mail` to legal person, per [ETSI EN 319 411-1/-2](https://www.etsi.org/deliver/etsi_en/319400_319499/).                                    | 🟡 Profiled |
| **Article 44(1)(c)** — identification of addressee before data delivery                                                         | Identification of the addressee before data is delivered.                                                                       | Qualified tier requires addressee `did:mail` to be certified (bound to a qualified certificate) prior to delivery. Non-certified recipients trigger `QTSP_UNAVAILABLE` non-delivery state.   | 🟡 Profiled |
| **Article 44(1)(d)** — data sending/receiving secured by advanced electronic signature or seal                                  | Sending and receiving secured by advanced electronic signature or advanced electronic seal.                                    | Ed25519 sender signature (advanced electronic signature under Article 26) + QTSP qualified seal (Article 36) on proof envelope.                                                               | ✅ Met      |
| **Article 44(1)(e)** — integrity guaranteed by qualified electronic signature or seal                                           | Integrity guaranteed by a qualified electronic signature or seal.                                                              | `ProofEnvelope` includes QTSP qualified seal over envelope hash at Qualified tier.                                                                                                           | ✅ Met      |
| **Article 44(1)(f)** — any change to data indicated                                                                             | Any change to the data is indicated.                                                                                           | BLAKE3 content hash + MLS transcript hash + CAdES-B-LTA archive timestamps for long-term detection.                                                                                          | ✅ Met      |
| **Article 44(1)(g)** — sending and receiving times indicated by qualified electronic time stamp                                 | Date and time of sending and receiving indicated by a qualified electronic time stamp.                                         | QTSP-issued qualified RFC 3161 timestamps on both deposit and read-receipt events; receipt required at Qualified tier (§6.4.3 of the founding specification).                                | ✅ Met      |
| **Article 44(1)(h)** — interoperability between QERDS                                                                           | Interoperability between different QERDS providers.                                                                            | Marque specifies canonical CBOR wire format + alignment with ETSI EN 319 522 semantic and EN 319 521 policy. Cross-implementation interop test matrix is a Phase-2 roadmap milestone.        | ⚠ Deferred  |

## 3. Adjacent qualified-trust-service provisions

Provisions that Qualified-tier Marque depends on but does not itself implement.

| eIDAS provision                                                                                                       | Marque dependency                                                                                                                                | Status      |
| --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ----------- |
| **Article 26** — requirements for advanced electronic signatures                                                      | Ed25519 sender signatures are advanced electronic signatures in the sense of Article 26. The `ProofEnvelope` carries the necessary attributes.   | ✅ Met      |
| **Article 28** — qualified certificates for electronic signatures                                                     | A Qualified-tier sender obtains a qualified certificate from a QTSP; the QTSP identifier appears in `proof_envelope.qtsp_cert`.                 | 🟡 Profiled |
| **Article 36** — requirements for advanced electronic seals                                                           | QTSP co-signature on the Marque `ProofEnvelope` is an advanced electronic seal. Upgraded to qualified seal when issued by a Qualified QTSP.      | ✅ Met      |
| **Article 42** — qualified electronic time stamps                                                                     | RFC 3161 timestamps issued by a Qualified QTSP satisfy Article 42 qualified-timestamp requirements. Attested tier uses non-qualified RFC 3161. | 🟡 Profiled |
| **Article 45a / 45c** — European Digital Identity Wallet interaction                                                  | `did:mail` identities MAY be attested by a European Digital Identity Wallet credential at the Qualified tier. Profile deferred to a future MIP. | ⚠ Deferred  |

## 5. Marque-specific deviations and extensions

Explicit places where Marque goes beyond, or narrows, the eIDAS / ETSI baseline.

| Deviation                                        | Detail                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        | Rationale                                                                                                                                                                             |
| ------------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **OpenTimestamps Bitcoin anchor**                | Marque Attested and Qualified tiers MAY additionally carry an OpenTimestamps `.ots` proof anchoring the envelope hash in Bitcoin. This is a Marque extension; eIDAS does not require it.                                                                                                      | Provides zero-cost, zero-trusted-party long-term timestamp verification. Survives any single QTSP or timestamping authority going out of business. Strictly additive to QERDS proof. |
| **Three-tier non-repudiation**                    | Marque splits what eIDAS treats as binary (ERDS vs QERDS) into three tiers: Casual (no proof), Attested (ERDS), Qualified (QERDS).                                                                                                                                                            | Casual tier carries no eIDAS claim and is the RECOMMENDED default for personal correspondence; selecting Qualified is an explicit per-message act with a cost quotation.             |
| **Sender confirms cost before Qualified send**   | Per §6.7b of the founding specification, a Qualified-tier send MUST present a cost quotation and MUST NOT commit without sender confirmation.                                                                                                                                                 | Qualified-tier messages carry non-trivial QTSP fees; surfacing them protects users from accidental expensive sends.                                                                   |
| **Qualified mutually exclusive with Mixed tier** | A Qualified-tier send MUST NOT select `privacy_tier = MIXED`. The envelope schema MUST-rejects the combination.                                                                                                                                                                                | A QTSP co-signature presupposes a named counterparty; mix-net delivery is incompatible.                                                                                                |
| **Read-receipt required at Qualified tier**      | Per §6.4.3, Qualified-tier messages REQUIRE a read-receipt path; the recipient's decline path is the machine-readable `REFUSED_TO_ACKNOWLEDGE` non-delivery state. Attested tier is opt-in; Casual tier is off.                                                                              | Mirrors PEC semantics (the Italian regulated QERDS-equivalent), ensuring acknowledgment is deliverable and auditable.                                                                |
| **Silent drop-to-spam prohibited**               | At all tiers, a provider MUST surface a machine-readable rejection reason rather than silently dropping. At Qualified tier, this is a hard MUST-reject requirement.                                                                                                                            | Aligned with eIDAS Article 44(1)(c) identification-before-delivery requirement and extends the discoverability guarantee to sub-Qualified tiers.                                       |

## 6. National QERDS-equivalents compatibility

Marque Qualified tier is designed to interoperate with existing national QERDS-equivalent systems during the Phase-2 and Phase-3 migration window.

| National system                                                               | Jurisdiction  | Marque interop posture                                                                                                                                                                                            | Status      |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------- |
| **PEC (Posta Elettronica Certificata)**                                       | Italy         | PEC is the concrete Phase-2 wedge. Marque Qualified tier targets feature parity with PEC, and a QTSP partnership is the anticipated first commercial deployment. PEC → Marque migration tooling is a Phase-2 deliverable. | 🟡 Profiled |
| **De-Mail**                                                                   | Germany       | De-Mail is effectively defunct: Deutsche Telekom exited in December 2022, federal-administration use ended 31 August 2024, and the remaining accredited providers wound down through 2024. Marque Qualified tier is the eIDAS-2.0-native successor for the De-Mail use-case. | ⚠ Deferred  |
| **eBoks**                                                                     | Denmark       | eBoks is a national digital-post system with a different architectural model (central repository); Marque Qualified can carry eBoks-equivalent semantics in a federated substrate.                                 | ⚠ Deferred  |
| **Lettre recommandée électronique (LRE)**                                     | France        | LRE is a registered-mail service regulated under French postal law and eIDAS-aligned. Marque Qualified tier can carry LRE semantics with a French QTSP partnership.                                                | ⚠ Deferred  |
| **SEPE / Carpeta Ciudadana**                                                  | Spain         | Citizen-to-administration secure messaging; Marque `org`-kind Rooms can serve as the legal-person endpoint.                                                                                                        | ⚠ Deferred  |
| **Cyber Fundamentals (BEC / BCE)**                                            | Belgium       | eIDAS-aligned; Marque Qualified applies directly.                                                                                                                                                                   | ⚠ Deferred  |

## 7. Path to formal conformance

1. **Phase 1 (current, 0–2 years).** Declarative mapping (this document). Example implementations under development (no privileged "reference" implementation; every conformant implementation is a peer). Stub Internet-Drafts under individual submission.
2. **Phase 2 (2–3 years).** Content-migrated Internet-Drafts (`draft-tamim-marque-proof`, `draft-tamim-marque-core`, `draft-tamim-marque-did-mail`). Partnership with a QTSP for the first QERDS-listed deployment. Initial self-assessment against **ETSI EN 319 521** (policy + security requirements) using the **TS 119 524** conformance test suite.
3. **Phase 2→3 transition.** Formal ETSI conformance assessment against EN 319 521 + TS 119 524-1/-2 + EN 319 522. Listing on a national trust list (Italy anticipated as first jurisdiction).
4. **Phase 3.** Cross-implementation interop test matrix published quarterly. Additional jurisdictions (France, Germany) onboard. ETSI EN 319 522-2 profile published covering Attested and Qualified-tier semantics.

See [`docs/roadmap.md`](../roadmap.md) for the full five-phase timeline.
