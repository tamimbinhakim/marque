# Code of conduct

Marque follows the **IETF Guidelines for Conduct** (BCP 54 / RFC 7154) and the **IETF Anti-Harassment Policy** (RFC 7776) with repository-specific additions below.

## Our standards

Participation in this project is a privilege extended on the basis that participants will:

- **Engage substantively.** Critique designs, cite evidence, propose alternatives. "I don't like it" is not an argument.
- **Stay on topic.** This repository governs a protocol specification. Political, commercial, or personal advocacy unrelated to the spec belongs elsewhere.
- **Disagree honestly and without personal attack.** Strong technical disagreement is welcome. Insults, harassment, and bad-faith rhetoric are not.
- **Respect the chair.** When editors call a discussion, work constructively within it. If you disagree with a decision, propose a MIP or appeal per [GOVERNANCE.md](./GOVERNANCE.md).
- **Disclose conflicts of interest.** If you represent a provider, vendor, regulator, or investor, say so the first time it becomes relevant in a thread.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Personal attacks, insults, harassment, or discriminatory language.
- Sexual attention, imagery, or language in any project venue.
- Threats of violence or legal action as a rhetorical device.
- Doxxing, including publishing private correspondence without consent.
- Sustained disruption of discussions or design meetings.
- Advocacy for deliberately weakening the protocol's security or privacy properties without explicit threat-model rationale (e.g. mandating key escrow) — this may be discussed once and declined; repeated re-proposal is disruption.

## Enforcement

> [!NOTE]
> **Pre-charter contact:** `conduct@marqueproto.org` is a reserved address that does not exist yet. Until it's live, conduct reports may be raised by opening a [private GitHub Security Advisory](https://github.com/tamimbinhakim/marque/security/advisories/new) on this repository — GitHub's PVR channel doubles as a private maintainer-contact channel pre-charter. Where the report concerns the founding editor, indicate that in the report; the editor will route to a neutral reviewer.

Violations should be reported to `conduct@marqueproto.org`. Reports are handled by a three-person Conduct Committee drawn from the editors plus one independent member, with a rotating chair. Decisions are made by simple majority. The subject of a report is notified and given 14 days to respond before any public action is taken, except in cases of ongoing harassment where a temporary measure may be issued immediately.

Sanctions, from least to most severe:

1. Private warning.
2. Public warning in the relevant thread.
3. Time-limited suspension from repository interaction (1 week to 6 months).
4. Permanent ban from the Marque project.

An editor found in violation is subject to the same scale, plus removal from editor role by editor supermajority (⅔ of non-accused editors).

## Appeals

Sanctions may be appealed by writing to `conduct@marqueproto.org`. Appeals are reviewed by a freshly constituted committee with no overlap with the original decision-makers.

## Scope

This code applies to all project venues: this repository, official mailing lists, meetings (in person or virtual), and any communication that identifies the participant as a Marque contributor.
